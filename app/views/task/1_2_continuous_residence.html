Check whether your time in the UK counts as continuous residence

To stay in the UK based on long residence, your time in the UK must be continuous.

Continuous residence means you have not left the UK for:

  • more than 180 days at a time
  • more than 540 days in total

Your 10-year qualifying period for long residence starts either when:

  • you first arrived in the UK with a visa
  • you were first given permission to stay in the UK

You can’t count time spent in:

  • a prison, young offender’s institution or secure hospital
  • the Republic of Ireland, Isle of Man or Channel Islands
