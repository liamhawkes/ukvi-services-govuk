Prove your knowledge of English

You might need to prove your knowledge of the English language if you’re aged 18 to 64 and applying to stay in the UK.

You can prove it by having either:

  • an English qualification at B1, B2, C1 or C2 level
  • a degree taught or researched in English

You don’t need to prove your knowledge of English in certain circumstances.

You can use a B1 level qualification that’s run out if both of the following are true:

  • it’s on the current list of recognised tests
  • it was accepted for another UK immigration application, for example when you got permission to enter

You can only use English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) qualifications if the course provider is on the approved list.

You can’t use other qualifications, for example GCSEs, A levels or National Vocational Qualifications (NVQs).
